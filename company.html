UP BUSINESS INCUBATOR FOR IT

The UP Cebu Business Incubator for Information Technology (CeBu InIT) is a technology business incubation center established by the University of the Philippines Cebu in partnership with the Department of Science and Technology (DOST). Located at the UP Cebu campus in Lahug, Cebu City, CeBu InIT aims to nurture and support technology-based startups, particularly in information and communication technology, creative media, and innovation-driven industries. It provides startup companies with office spaces, mentoring, business development training, networking opportunities, and access to university resources such as faculty expertise, student interns, and research facilities. The incubator also collaboratese with the UP Cebu FabLab to help entrepreneurs develop prototypes and bring their ideas to market. Since its founding in 2010, CeBu InIT has successfully supported numerous startups, many of which have graduated and become self-sustaining businesses. It continues to serve as a hub for innovation and entrepreneurship in the Visayas Region.

I also learned that teamwork and proper planning are important in building a project. The visit showed me that a simple idea can grow into something successful with the right support.

T.a.R.S.I.E.R 117

T.A.R.S.I.E.R. 117

T.A.R.S.I.E.R. 117 Which stands for Tourist Assistance and Rescue Section, Immediate Emergency Responders, is an emergency response and disaster management unit established by the Provincial Government of Bohol. It was created to ensure the safety and security of both locals and tourists by providing immediate assistance during emergencies, such as accidents, natural disasters, and medical situations. Operating 24/7 through the emergency hotline 117, T.A.R.S.I.E.R. 117 is composed of trained responders equipped for rescue, first aid, and disaster operations.

The unit also works closely with the Philippine National Police(PNP), Bureau of Fire Protection(BFP), and Department of Tourism(DOT) to strengthen public safety and promote a secure environment for visitors in Bohol.

My Observation

During our visit to T.A.R.S.I.E.R. 117, I saw how serious and prepared they are when it comes to emergencies. Their team explained how they handle accidents, disasters, and medical cases. They also showed some of their equipment and the vehicles they use for rescue. I noticed that everyone works with focus and coordination, and they are always ready since they operate day and night. They also work closely with other groups like the police and fire department to make sure people in Bohol are safe.

My Learnings

I learned that emergency response is not easy and requires training, discipline, and teamwork. I also learned that safety is a shared responsibility, and groups like T.A.R.S.I.E.R. 117 play a big role in keeping both tourists and locals safe. Their work reminded me that being prepared can save lives, especially during disasters or sudden accidents. I understood the importance of quick action, clear communication, and staying calm during emergencies.
